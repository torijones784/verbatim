About Tori Jones

Why hire a coder MBA to do a writer's job?

I've made my career out of saying yes to things

When I was 22, the startup I worked for asked me to learn how to implement a business intelligence tool from scratch with no experience. I said yes, and that's how I learned to code.

When I was 24, a boutique consulting agency brought me on to project manage some of their top clients - including a Fortune 10 tech company and a top 4 telecommunications firm. I said yes, and that's how I learned enterprise-level project management and stakeholder communication.

My career has been about bridging the gap between "what if" and "here it is." From building automated business intelligence systems at hyper-growth startups to designing operational improvements at major telecom companies, I specialize in making ambitious ideas achievable.

At Cresco Labs, I took a 75-person cannabis startup through 20x growth by implementing their first real-time reporting system - handling 54+ million rows across 1000+ datasets and cutting projected analytics costs by 65%. I taught myself to code, built the entire infrastructure, and delivered insights directly to C-suite executives.

For Tilt Holdings, I led end-to-end business analytics transformation across their 4-brand cannabis portfolio, designing automated forecasting systems that saved 10 working days monthly across sales, marketing, finance, and operations teams. I freed teams from manual data wrestling so they could focus on strategic growth.

I'm an MBA from University of Chicago Booth and a technical resource you can actually talk to. I've self-taught all of my technical knowledge, which means I approach problems from a human-first, not tech-first perspective.

In my spare time, I work on Pixel & Prose - my exploration into how technology can make reading more exciting through interactive fiction that puts millennial women at the center of immersive digital storytelling.

I'm looking for a role that will allow me to bring my full self to the job - my strategic thinking & operational improvement skills, my technical abilities, and my never-ending drive to make the big, hairy ideas conceivable & achievable.

This might sound strange, but I'm very comfortable with mess. To me, mess is inevitable in transformational work - and by embracing it, we can build things that actually work for real people.
